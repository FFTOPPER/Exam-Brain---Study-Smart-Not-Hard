Problem : Past paper analyzer that maps topic frequency against syllabus, ranks
high-yield topics, and generates a smart study planner
Problem Statement
Students often rely on past question papers to prepare for exams but lack a structured
way to analyze trends and identify important topics. Manually reviewing multiple years
of papers is time-consuming and fails to reveal deeper patterns or question frequency
across topics. As a result, students struggle to prioritize high-impact topics and prepare
strategically, leading to inefficient study sessions and lower exam performance. Build an
AI-powered system that analyzes past papers and syllabus to identify patterns, predict
important topics, and guide smarter exam preparation.
Students / users often struggle with:
● Identifying which topics appear most frequently across multiple years of question
papers.
● Understanding question patterns — phrasing styles, difficulty distribution, and
marking schemes.
● Aligning study priorities with the actual exam syllabus and historical trends.
● Spending too much time on low-yield topics while missing high-frequency
concepts.
To solve this, build an AI-powered app that allows users to get:
● Multi-paper upload — accept past question papers (PDF/image) spanning multiple
years and subjects.
● AI pattern analysis — identify topic frequency, question type distribution, and
year-wise trends.
● Syllabus cross-referencing — map extracted topics against the official syllabus to
● highlight coverage gaps.
● Topic importance scoring — rank topics by predicted exam weight based on
historical patterns.
● Smart study planner — auto-generate a prioritized study schedule based on
analysis results.
● Visual analytics dashboard — charts showing topic frequency, difficulty trends,
and coverage heatmaps.
● Practice question suggestions — recommend similar questions for high-priority
topics.
